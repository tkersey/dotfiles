# Dominance standard

A move is dominant only if it materially beats plausible alternatives on at least one major axis while not losing badly on the others.

## Major axes

- capability expansion
- future-work compression
- invariant strengthening
- proof or measurement value
- interface simplification
- reversibility and diff discipline
- implementation risk concentration
- time to first trustworthy proof
- blast-radius reduction
- decision unblock value

## Gates

Eliminate or heavily penalize candidates that fail these gates:

- **Evidence gate**: payoff is not grounded in current artifact state.
- **Proof gate**: no credible first proof signal.
- **Diff gate**: change surface is broad relative to payoff.
- **Theater gate**: ambition does not improve leverage, proof, or optionality.
- **Constraint gate**: move violates a hard constraint or relies on a decision not yet made.
- **Reversibility gate**: move creates hard-to-unwind structure without commensurate payoff.

## Verdicts

Use exactly one:

- `Winner`
- `No dominant move`
- `Insufficient evidence`
