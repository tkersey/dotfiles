# Ablative Clearance

Ablative clearance prevents a valid review comment from automatically becoming an
additive patch.

## Principle

A selected action must preserve the live contract while reducing or justifying the
total semantic surface. Additive mutation is permitted only after lower-surface
routes are defeated with current artifact evidence.

## Doctrine words

- **ablative**: remove accumulated surface while preserving the living structure.
- **canonicalizing**: choose one owner, representation, path, or proof surface.
- **dominated**: another route covers the obligation with lower complexity or better proof.
- **subsumed**: the obligation is already owned by a broader canonical path.
- **vestigial**: the obligation once existed but is no longer live.
- **uninhabited**: the branch/state cannot occur if constructors and invariants are sound.
- **deforesting**: remove pass-through wrappers, adapters, and intermediate structures.
- **conservative**: preserve public behavior and compatibility unless retirement is explicit.

## Clearance outcomes

- `clear-additive`: deletion/collapse/reuse/canonicalization is defeated; additive mutation is warranted.
- `select-ablative-route`: lower-surface route should replace additive mutation.
- `validate-first`: reachability or obligation status must be proven before mutation.
- `veto-additive`: additive mutation is dominated or broadens surface unjustifiably.
- `unresolved`: evidence is insufficient to license mutation.
- `not-required`: no mutation-capable route is selected.

## Required proof

For deletion/collapse/canonicalization, require at least one:

- reachability/import evidence
- test/proof that behavior remains preserved
- proof that the obligation moved to a canonical owner
- proof that the state/branch is uninhabited under current constructors/invariants
- public-contract check showing no promised behavior is retired silently
- migration/compatibility check when historical data or external callers exist


## Isomorphic safety addendum

Lower-surface routes are not automatically safe. Deletion, collapse, reuse, and canonicalization require an Ablative Isomorphism Card or a `validate-first` warrant. Apparent duplication must be clone-classified before merge: exact, parametric, gapped, semantic, or accidental-rhyme. Semantic clones and accidental rhymes do not collapse without equivalence proof.
