# Ablative Clearance

Ablative clearance prevents a valid concern from becoming additive code by
momentum.

A selected mutation-capable route must preserve the live contract while reducing
or justifying total semantic surface. Additive mutation is permitted only after
lower-surface routes are defeated with current artifact evidence.

Clearance outcomes:

- `select-ablative-route`: deletion/collapse/reuse/canonicalization should be the route
- `clear-additive`: lower-surface routes are defeated and additive mutation is warranted
- `validate-first`: reachability/equivalence/obligation must be proven before mutation
- `veto-additive`: additive mutation is dominated or broadens surface unjustifiably
- `unresolved`: evidence is insufficient to license mutation
- `not-required`: no mutation-capable route is selected

Deletion, collapse, merge, reuse, and canonicalization require an Ablative
Isomorphism Card or a validate-first warrant.
