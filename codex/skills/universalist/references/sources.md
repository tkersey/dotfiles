## Claim boundaries

“Comonads as Spaces” is a July 2026 v1 preprint. Treat software mappings as research-informed architecture inference.

Tambara-module results are established under their stated hypotheses. Treat mappings to middleware, Exact Context, halos, repository architecture, and agents as engineering interpretations unless the ambient action, profunctor, framing laws, and representation assumptions are modeled.

Double categories, equipments, structured cospans, and double-functorial semantics are established constructions under stated hypotheses. Treat mappings to repository refactors, migration proof leases, software-change calculus, query transport, and architecture-evolution squares as engineering interpretations unless the code models both arrow families, squares, both pastings, interchange/coherence, and effective interpretation.

Mark nontrivial claims as mathematical, programming, architecture inference, or repo observation.

For double-category claims distinguish:

```text
literal strict/pseudo/virtual double category or equipment
effective repository-native realization of arrow/square/pasting laws
two-dimensional diagram analogy only
```

Require:

```text
two semantically distinct arrow families
identity and composition in both directions
typed squares with four matching boundaries
horizontal and vertical square pasting
interchange or explicit coherent comparison
interpreter/double-functor preservation
effective normalization, resources, and invalidation
```

Do not call two categories, one commuting square, a PROP diagram, or a DPO rewrite a double category. Do not infer effect commutativity from interchange. Do not claim equipment without companions, conjoints, or restrictions and their laws.

For pullback/pushout, comonadic spatiality, Day/promonoidal convolution, and Tambara claims, preserve their existing literal/effective/analogy distinctions and require their named universal, coherence, interpretation, and effectivity obligations.

The term **Tambara** is overloaded. Distinguish profunctor/module-category Tambara modules from equivariant Tambara functors and Tambara-Yamagami categories.
