# Law discovery and non-laws

A law is a proposed equality or preservation rule under a chosen observation boundary. A non-law is a tempting equality that fails under some relevant observation.

## Observation-relative equality

Before claiming a law, name the observation:

```text
Under item-quantity observation, cart merge may be commutative.
Under audit-trace observation, the same merge may not be commutative.
```

Law claims should include:

```text
Carrier:
Operation(s):
Observation:
Law:
Why expected:
Property test:
Counterexample search:
```

Non-laws should include:

```text
Tempting law:
Failed observation:
Counterexample:
Architecture consequence:
```

## Common law families

- identity;
- associativity;
- commutativity;
- idempotency;
- absorption / annihilation;
- monotonicity;
- fusion;
- round trip;
- preservation;
- totality;
- compensation rather than inverse;
- ordering/effect non-commutation.

## Universalist use

Non-laws are high-value design data. They often reveal:

- missing state variants;
- effect-ordering requirements;
- audit/trace observability;
- compensation instead of inverse;
- stronger observation vocabularies;
- exact-context missingness or contradiction;
- need for Freyd, operadic, coalgebraic, or sheafification mechanics.
