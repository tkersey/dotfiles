# Depth and Deliberation Doctrine

Use this reference when a task needs more than generic `think harder` language.

The governing distinction is:

```text
EXCAVATORY -> descend through causal, representational, historical, and invariant layers
APORETIC   -> remain productively inside an unresolved difficulty until its real structure is explicit
```

These operators are adjacent but not interchangeable.

## `excavatory`

Descend beneath the visible symptom, first explanation, or inherited frame until the next layer changes the model, owner, intervention, or proof obligation.

Use when:

- the first diagnosis is symptom-shaped;
- hidden assumptions or historical layers may govern the outcome;
- a local defect may be downstream of a deeper invariant or ownership break;
- the agent needs permission to investigate below the obvious layer.

Cash-out when correctness, handoff, or closure needs it:

```md
Excavation Trace:
- surface symptom:
- proximate mechanism:
- enabling condition:
- hidden assumption:
- governing invariant:
- owning boundary:
- route delta:
- stop reason:
```

Stop when another layer no longer changes the explanatory model, route, owner, or proof burden.

Failure modes:

- depth theater that only lengthens the explanation;
- endless causal regress;
- ignoring a simple dispositive fact because a deeper story sounds smarter;
- excavating beyond the task's evidence or authority.

## `aporetic`

Remain productively inside a genuine difficulty, contradiction, or underdetermination rather than forcing premature closure.

Use when:

- multiple serious frames remain live;
- each apparent solution preserves a material contradiction;
- requirements, evidence, or values are genuinely in tension;
- the agent is likely to collapse uncertainty into confident prose.

Cash-out when a decision boundary needs to be shared:

```md
Aporia Map:
- unresolved difficulty:
- live positions:
- contradiction or tension:
- evidence supporting each position:
- missing distinction or evidence:
- what would dissolve the aporia:
- current disposition: investigate | experiment | decide-under-uncertainty | blocked
```

Aporia is productive only while it sharpens the decision surface. It must not become indecision theater.

Failure modes:

- indefinite rumination;
- treating ordinary uncertainty as philosophical depth;
- refusing a reversible decision despite sufficient evidence;
- using ambiguity to avoid ownership.

## Adjacent operators

| Word | Activated behavior | Distinction |
|---|---|---|
| `ruminative` | keep turning the material over | broader and less structured than `aporetic` |
| `dialectical` | confront competing frames and resolve their tension | expects opposition and synthesis |
| `circumspective` | inspect the problem from every relevant side | breadth around the object, not depth beneath it |
| `etiological` | trace a failure to its causal origin | narrower root-cause operator |
| `stratigraphic` | read historical layers and the obligations they once carried | best for technical debt and compatibility sediment |
| `foundational` | descend to premises, axioms, and definitions | best for conceptual or specification errors |
| `synoptic` | reassemble the parts into one coherent whole | usually follows excavation or dialectic |
| `dispositive` | identify the layer or fact that actually decides the issue | stopping companion for excavation and aporia |

## Strong stacks

### Root-cause work

```text
EXCAVATORY + ETIOLOGICAL + DISPOSITIVE
```

Descend through the layers, identify the causal origin, and stop at the layer that changes the remedy.

### High-judgment ambiguity

```text
APORETIC + DIALECTICAL + ADJUDICATIVE
```

Keep the real difficulty open, make the serious frames confront one another, then issue a criteria-backed disposition.

### Deep systems diagnosis

```text
CIRCUMSPECTIVE + EXCAVATORY + CYBERNETIC + SYNOPTIC
```

Inspect the whole system, descend into the governing mechanism, model feedback, and reconstruct the coherent whole.

### Thought-to-action

```text
APORETIC + EXCAVATORY + DISPOSITIVE + ACTUATING
```

Do not force closure, descend to the decisive layer, identify what governs, then move the system.

## Activation forms

When the surrounding workflow already owns evidence and stopping rules, keep the runtime activation terse:

```text
Be excavatory.
Be aporetic.
```

Use the expanded artifacts only when the user asks for unpacking or when correctness, handoff, or closure depends on them.

## Distinction rules

- `excavatory` is not synonymous with verbose, exhaustive, or speculative.
- `aporetic` is not synonymous with confused, indecisive, or blocked.
- `etiological` asks where the cause originates; `excavatory` may descend through causal, representational, historical, or ownership layers.
- `ruminative` sustains reconsideration; `aporetic` names a specific unresolved difficulty that must not be prematurely collapsed.
- `dispositive` is a natural stopping companion: identify what actually decides the matter.
