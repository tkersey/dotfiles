# Doctrine Alpha Reference

Doctrine words improve frontier-agent performance when they become executable surfaces:

- gates
- ledgers
- route choices
- validators
- proof receipts
- selection maps
- fixed-point loops
- authority packets
- surface delta receipts
- required tail summaries

Words alone mainly increase style, confidence, and verbosity.

## Keep high-value doctrine

- `right-sized`
- `surface-accounted`
- `governing invariant`
- `canonical owner`
- `traceable`
- `witness-bearing`
- `untrusted-until-witnessed`
- `unwitnessed guarantee`
- `illegal inhabitant`
- `tail proof`

## Define accretive carefully

`accretive` means the delivered artifact has more durable value after surface accounting.

It does not mean additive.

A deletion, collapse, canonicalization, stricter constructor, narrower type, better witness, or no-code proof can be the most accretive move.

## Demote when not operationalized

- generic `rigor`
- ornamental `adversarial`
- isolated `mechanistic`
- vague `unsound`
- vague `accretive`
- `de novo` without candidate inventory and no-finding countercase
- `ablation` without a concrete delete/collapse/canonicalize candidate or not-required receipt

## Minimum cash-out

```text
What artifact does this word create?
What evidence fills that artifact?
What decision changes because of it?
What would falsify or close it?
What surface does it add, retire, or preserve?
```
