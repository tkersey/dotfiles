# System Types and DART

## Four system types

### Clear

Cause and effect are obvious.

Use checklist, standard work, precise execution.

### Complicated

Cause and effect exist but are hidden.

Use analysis, decomposition, and the right expert.

### Complex

Cause and effect emerge over time and are visible mostly in hindsight.

Use safe-to-fail experiments, monitoring, adaptation, and humility.

### Chaotic

Cause and effect are broken or unknowable in time.

Act first to stabilize and create safety. Analyze afterward.

## DART

```text
D — Deconstruct: parts, stocks, flows, boundaries.
A — Analyze: cause/effect relationship and system type.
R — Recognize: known patterns, analogues, archetypes.
T — Test: smallest safe probe; in chaos, stabilize first.
```

## Mixed systems

Split mixed systems by subsystem.

A technical subsystem may be complicated while adoption is complex and incident response is chaotic.
