# Incentives and Cobra Effects

Before changing rewards, metrics, rules, or targets, ask:

```text
What behavior does this reward?
What proxy replaces the real goal?
Who can game it?
What becomes invisible?
Who pays the cost?
What delayed harm appears after the metric improves?
```

## Cobra-effect risk

High risk when:

- reward is attached to a proxy;
- actors can create the measured thing;
- the measured thing is easier to game than the goal;
- there is weak feedback from true system health;
- delayed harms arrive after the reward is paid.

## Safer design

- measure the real stock when possible;
- combine leading and lagging indicators;
- monitor gaming signals;
- give feedback to decision-makers quickly;
- revise incentives when unintended behavior appears.
